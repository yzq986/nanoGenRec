## Discover the source

Paper 2603.01683 "Surgical Post-Training: Cutting Errors, Keeping Knowledge" (SPOT, University of Hong Kong, 2026-03)

## Core Discovery

### 1. Structural flaws of DPO

SPOT theoretically reveals why standard DPOs perform poorly on **rigid correctness** tasks (clearly defined correctness/incorrectness):

The gradient path of DPO is "minimize margin M = r(y+) - r(y-)", so the most labor-saving optimization method is to lower r(y-) rather than increase r(y+). In the experiment, it was observed that the chosen reward of DPO stopped increasing but the rejected reward continued to decrease in the late training period.

This corresponds closely to our SP-DPO scenario:
- y- = wrong SID sequence generated by beam-search
- y+ = teacher-forced target SID sequence
- The goal is to make the model more likely to generate target SID, rather than just "better than beam"

If SP-DPO optimizes for margin rather than absolute correctness, the model may satisfy the loss by reducing confidence in the beam results, rather than actually improving the probability of generating the target SID.

### 2. SPoT-BCO is a better alternative

SPOT proposes to decouple DPO into **independent two categories**:

```
L_BCO = -E[log σ(r(x,y+) - δ) + log σ(-(r(x,y-) - δ))]
```

- **δ** = exponential moving average of batch reward (to prevent the gradient from disappearing when r increases)
- Independently maximize y+ confidence + minimize y- confidence
- Compared with DPO, "Elastic Tether" is additionally introduced: when r(y+) is large enough, the gradient automatically disappears to prevent over-optimization

SPOT experiment: SPoT-BCO outperforms DPO by an average of +3.5% on reasoning tasks without causing catastrophic forgetting.

### 3. Data quality filtering

LCS filtering (RLCS < 0.6) ensures that y+ and y- only diverge at critical locations. Corresponding to our scenario:

```
RLCS(beam_sid, target_sid) = 1 - |LCS(beam_tokens, target_tokens)| / |target_tokens|
```

Only keep samples where beam and target partially match (RLCS < 0.6):
- Completely different beam (RLCS ≈ 1.0) = low information content, gradients concentrated on global rather than local errors
- Partial matching (RLCS 0.2~0.6) = The error is in the middle and rear layer SID token, and the gradient is accurately positioned
