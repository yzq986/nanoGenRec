## EXP-048: M-tier + TO-RoPE — 2-dim vs 3-dim (order/time/layer)
[English](exp-048.md) | [Chinese](exp-048.zh.md)


**Date**: 2026-04-30
**Status**: completed

### Background
EXP-044B Verification TO-RoPE is valid in S-tier (+2.4pp). EXP-047 L-tier gains masked by lack of training. This experiment returns to M-tier and tests 2-dim vs 3-dim RoPE (new layer_id dimension).

### Design
- **Model**: M-tier (scale-06, embed_dim=512, 8L, 8E top-2 MoE, 71.6M active)
- **Features**: segment_emb + time_gap + action_level + gate_attn + TO-RoPE
- **Data**: exp044b-0.6b-14d; SID: exp026-0.6b-14d; LR: 1e-3
- **Baseline**: exp043-m-0.6b (R@500=70.2%, PPL=18.54)

| Variant | rope_dims |
|---------|-----------|
| exp048-m-2dim | order:0.5, time:0.5 |
| exp048-m-3dim | order:0.4, time:0.5, layer:0.1 |

### Results

| Config | R@10 | R@500 | PPL |
|--------|------|-------|-----|
| exp043-m-0.6b (baseline) | 14.5% | 70.2% | 18.54 |
| exp048-m-2dim | 15.1% | **70.1%** | 17.67 |
| exp048-m-3dim | 15.5% | 69.5% | 17.64 |

### Analysis

1. **TO-RoPE has no profit in M-tier**: 2-dim is the same as baseline (-0.1pp), and 3-dim drops slightly (-0.7pp). The stronger capacity of M-tier has fully captured the timing through absolute position encoding, and the marginal contribution of RoPE disappears.
2. **3-dim < 2-dim**: layer:0.1 occupies the head dimension of order (0.5→0.4), R@10 is higher but R@500 is lower.
3. **PPL both improved** (17.6 vs 18.54), there was a gain in language modeling but it did not translate into R@500.
4. **Conclusion**: TO-RoPE is not recommended for use in M-tier + 1 epoch configuration.
